Bull Skill: The Hidden Dimension

18 billion simulated games reveal that your bull multiplier changes which strategy you should play

The Hidden Variable

Every darts player knows their MPR. But almost nobody thinks about how well they hit bull relative to regular numbers. Our research shows this is a critical oversight.

We simulated 18 billion games across 12 asymmetric bull multiplier combinations, 121 skill pairings, and all 22 strategies. The results are unambiguous: your bull multiplier is not noise — it fundamentally changes which strategy you should play.

The key finding: A +0.25 bull multiplier advantage creates an ~8 percentage point spread between the favored and unfavored player, with the advantaged side gaining most of the edge. At equal MPR, a 1.00x bull player beats a 0.25x bull opponent ~65% of the time — using completely different strategies. The effect scales with skill: ~6pp spread at low MPR, up to ~12pp at pro level.

The bull multiplier scales your normal hit probabilities when aiming at bull. A 0.75x multiplier means if you hit triples 30% on regular numbers, your equivalent accuracy on bull drops to ~22.5%. A 1.00x multiplier means bull is no harder than numbers for you.

What the Data Shows

Strategy Depends on Bull

The optimal strategy significantly changes based on bull accuracy (chi-squared = 246.3, p < 0.0001). Bad at bull? Defer it — play S9 (high threshold) or E2 (honeypot). Good at bull? Exploit it with E1 (early bull closure). This isn't a minor adjustment — it's a completely different approach.

Bull Matters More at High Skill

At low MPR, bull accuracy barely matters — misses dominate everything. From MPR 2.0 upward, a +0.25 bull advantage creates a ~7–8pp spread between players. At pro level (5.6), that spread grows to ~11pp. Better players reach the bull-closure phase faster, making accuracy there decisive.

Bull = Hidden MPR

Each +0.25 bull multiplier step compensates for ~0.13 MPR on average. But this scales with skill: at 4.0 MPR, a 0.75 multiplier gap is worth 0.40 MPR — the difference between a good league player and a semi-pro.

Every Comparison is Significant

With 20,000 games per matchup, every single one of the 2,904 pairwise comparisons shows a statistically significant bull effect (all p < 0.0001). 95% confidence intervals are ±0.7%.

How This Works

Recommendations are drawn from a tournament of 22 strategies competing across all combinations of:

  • 11 MPR levels (0.8 to 5.6) for each player
  • 4 bull multiplier levels (0.25x, 0.50x, 0.75x, 1.00x) for each player
  • 20,000 games per strategy matchup

Total: approximately 18 billion simulated games. Each recommendation is backed by at least 20,000 games of direct evidence. See Methodology for full simulation details and Strategies for what each strategy does.

Based on Frongello (2018), "Optimal Strategy in Darts Cricket." Simulation code and data available on GitHub.
